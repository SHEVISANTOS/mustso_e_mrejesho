# MUST e-Mrejesho User Manual

## 1. What this system is

MUST e-Mrejesho is a digital accountability and feedback platform for students and student government representatives at Mbeya University of Science and Technology (MUST).

The system helps users to:

- submit complaints, suggestions, inquiries, or compliments
- track the progress of each case
- allow department representatives to respond and resolve issues
- allow administrators to monitor the whole system
- record public campaign promises and official documents
- get notifications about updates

---

## 2. Who can use the system

The site has three main user roles:

### Student
- Can register an account
- Can submit feedback
- Can view only their own submitted cases
- Can track the status of their case using the tracking number

### Representative
- Works for a specific department
- Can view assigned feedback cases in their department
- Can add comments, escalate cases, and resolve them
- Can create and update campaign promises
- Can upload public documents

### Admin
- Has access to the full system
- Can monitor all feedback items
- Can view analytics and department performance
- Can create or manage representative/admin accounts
- Can create and activate or deactivate departments

---

## 3. How to access the site

1. Open the site in your browser.
2. The homepage redirects to the login page.
3. Use your username and password to sign in.
4. If you do not have an account, use the registration page to create one.

### Login page
- Use your account details to sign in.
- Students and representatives can use the main login page.
- Admin users can also access the dedicated admin login page.

### Registration page
Students should register using their official student/staff account details.

---

## 4. Student guide

### Step 1: Create an account
1. Go to the registration page.
2. Fill in your required details.
3. Choose a secure password.
4. Submit the form.

### Step 2: Log in
1. Go to the login page.
2. Enter your username and password.
3. Click Login.

### Step 3: Submit feedback
1. Open the dashboard.
2. Click the Submit Feedback button.
3. Choose the appropriate category:
   - Complaint
   - Suggestion
   - Inquiry
   - Compliment
4. Select the correct department.
5. Add a subject and description.
6. If needed, choose to submit anonymously.
7. Click Submit.

### Step 4: Track your case
After submitting, the system gives you a tracking number such as:

`MREJ-2026-705902`

Use this number to identify and monitor your case.

### Step 5: View updates
On your dashboard, you can:
- see the status of your submissions
- view the latest updates
- open the full case details page
- receive notification alerts when movement occurs on your case

### Important note
Students only see their own cases. They cannot access or manage feedback submitted by other users.

---

## 5. Representative guide

### Step 1: Log in
Use your representative account details.

### Step 2: Open your dashboard
After login, you will see all feedback cases relevant to your department.

### Step 3: Review new cases
You can:
- check new feedback submitted to your department
- see its priority and status
- open the case detail page

### Step 4: Add comments
On the case detail page, you can:
- add a public comment
- update the case status in progress
- provide a response to the student

### Step 5: Escalate a case
If a case requires higher attention, you can escalate it to admin level.

### Step 6: Resolve a case
When the issue has been addressed, you can mark the case as resolved.

### Step 7: Close a case
After confirmation, the case can be closed as completed.

### Step 8: Manage promises
Representatives can create or update campaign promises through the Promises section.

### Step 9: Upload documents
Representatives can upload public documents such as meeting minutes, official notices, and reports.

---

## 6. Admin guide

### Accessing the Admin Panel
Admins see an Admin Panel button in the top navigation bar.

### Main admin functions
From the admin panel, an admin can:
- view an analytics dashboard
- see total cases, resolution rate, and average resolution time
- review department performance
- manage departments
- create representative and admin accounts
- activate or deactivate staff accounts

### Analytics dashboard
The analytics page shows:
- total number of cases
- resolved cases
- escalated cases
- resolution rate
- average turnaround time
- department activity
- charts for status, category, and department trends

### Department management
Admins can:
- create a new department
- edit department details
- activate or deactivate a department

### Staff account management
Admins can:
- create new staff accounts
- assign them to departments
- activate or suspend staff accounts

> Only admins should use the in-app Admin Panel for day-to-day operational management.

---

## 7. Notifications and alerts

The system uses an in-app notification bell and email alerts.

Users receive notifications when:
- a new case is submitted
- a case is commented on
- a case is escalated
- a case is resolved
- a case is closed

### How to check notifications
1. Click the bell icon in the top-right of the page.
2. Review the notification list.
3. Use the unread badge to know when new alerts arrive.

---

## 8. Promises and public accountability

The Promises section is used to track campaign commitments and accountability goals.

### What you can do here
- view a public dashboard of promises
- filter by status or category
- open the detail page of any promise
- review progress and evidence

### Representative role in promises
Representatives and admins can create promises, update their progress, and attach proof where needed.

---

## 9. Documents section

The Documents section is a searchable public archive.

You can:
- browse uploaded documents
- search by title or description
- filter by document type

Only representatives and admins can upload public documents.

---

## 10. Common terminology

- Feedback: a complaint, suggestion, inquiry, or compliment submitted by a student
- Tracking number: unique case reference generated when a case is submitted
- Escalation: moving a case to a higher level for action
- Resolution: marking the issue as handled
- Representative: department-level staff member responsible for first response
- Admin: system-wide manager with full access

---

## 11. Best practice for users

### For students
- Submit clear descriptions
- Use the correct department
- Keep the tracking number safe
- Check notifications regularly

### For representatives
- Respond promptly to incoming cases
- Add useful comments for clarity
- Escalate cases when needed
- Mark cases resolved only when they are truly addressed

### For admins
- Review analytics regularly
- Keep departments active and accurate
- Verify staff account roles
- Monitor unresolved or escalated cases

---

## 12. Troubleshooting

### I cannot log in
- Confirm that your username and password are correct.
- Make sure your account is active.
- If you are an admin, use the admin login route if necessary.

### I do not see any feedback cases
- Check whether the case belongs to your department.
- Ensure you are logged in with the correct role.
- Students only see their own submissions.

### I do not receive a notification
- Check the bell icon.
- Confirm that the notification was linked to your account.
- If you do not see it immediately, refresh the page.

### I cannot create or edit promises/documents
- Only representatives and admins have upload rights.
- If your account is not linked to the right department or role, contact an admin.

---

## 13. Summary

MUST e-Mrejesho is designed to make student feedback and accountability simple, visible, and traceable.

- Students submit and track cases.
- Representatives respond, escalate, and resolve them.
- Admins monitor the full system through analytics and staff management.

If used correctly, the system provides a transparent workflow from submission to resolution.
